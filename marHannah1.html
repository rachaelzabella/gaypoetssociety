Home, again 


On my lips is the taste of summer
The cloud of strawberry air
And my mother is sitting there, in the corner
Where she has always sat. I wonder
Did she sit there when I was gone? 
In our haunted house.

Too full of memories are these rooms,
Too many cobwebs hang in corners. 
The paint layered on paint, on paint,
It is not a means to cover the cracks-
Cannot heal the wound.

The mother holds the home,
Cradling the corpse for me, keeping house.
Rooms unused, space occupied by nothing-
Nothing but absence and shadows.
