partial eclipse 


now i live in festering fruit cans, the lid cuts me on the way out,
and i watch myself in your half-eclipsed face.
we killed our mother in that fire –
that fire that sparked when i burned the cross,
and a cross from me he wept and said
i pray for you, when i wish you were dead,
but neither of us have spoken to god in quite some time.
	 			i was your lover – a year passed – a killer,
hidden in post-apocalyptic shells,
hearts disconnected like prematurely pulled iv drips
and dripping from my eyes acid rain burns my skin –
burns like that fire,
that fire.
	 			a year passed – a year is too much 
for a life cut and shaven to fit inside these fingerprints
that we share, too much to bear the changing familiarity
in your half-scarred face, a year, a year.
the ashy flame sowed something rotting from the roots,
parasitical, planting pulmonary pain and causing caustic cardiac
silence.
give it time, give it time,
but time only hardens the moon rock embracing us both.
	 			a year passed – and
now i live in festering fruit cans,
